



What are English vowels?
A vowel is a particular kind of speech sound made by changing the shape of the upper vocal tract, or the area in the mouth above the tongue. ... 
These letters are vowels in English: A, E, I, O, U, and sometimes Y.
 It is said that Y is "sometimes" a vowel, because the letter Y represents both vowel and consonant sounds


--[English vowels]--

--[A -or- AN]--

AN --> next word start with vowels(A, E, I, O, U)
AN Apple
AN Egg

A --> next word start without vowels(A, E, I, O, U)
A Book
A Table

--[SOME / ANY]--

SOME --> positive sentence
ANY --> negative sentence
